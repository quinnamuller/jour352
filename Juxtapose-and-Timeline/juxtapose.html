White Plains, NY development over nearly 150 years

1776 vs. 1920

The City of White Plains lies in Westchester County, New York, just north of New York City. The first map shows its development in terms of roadways in 1776. The map, sourced from the Westchester County Historical Society, dates back to the Battle of White Plains which resulted in the retreat of George Washington's Continental Army. In 1920, the area was much more developed, especially as the suburban area developed. Today, White Plains is more urban than not, and is littered with skyscrapers.
